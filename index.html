Research Interests

My research centers on understanding where and why antitumor immune responses fail in solid tumors, and how delivery platforms can be rationally designed to intervene across multiple nodes of immune failure. I am particularly interested in DC biology, mRNA-LNP delivery, neoantigen targeting, and TME-mediated suppression.

Question 01
Where does the antitumor immune response break down in solid tumors — at the level of T cell priming, or in the infiltration and persistence of primed T cells within the tumor microenvironment?
Despite extensive clinical investigation of DC-based vaccines, the relative contribution of priming failure versus TME-mediated suppression has not been mechanistically isolated in the literature — a gap that directly motivated my interest in pursuing doctoral training in cancer immunology.
Question 02
How can therapeutic delivery platforms be designed to intervene synergistically across multiple bottlenecks in antitumor immunity?
My doctoral research aims to move beyond single-node interventions. My background in lipid-polymer nanoparticles and mRNA delivery, combined with a deeper mechanistic understanding of immunological systems I hope to build through doctoral training, positions me to design platforms that bridge delivery engineering and cancer immunology — coordinating DC activation, neoantigen presentation, and T cell persistence within a unified therapeutic framework.
